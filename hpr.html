High Power Rocketry

Aerospace Engineering Applications

Pictured: Liftoff of my Sepetron Rocket, Feburary 2020.

High power rocketry involves the design, construction, and flight of rockets using motors with more than 160 Newton seconds of impulse. Unlike in model rocketry, high power rocket motors require certifications to purchase and fly. At a base level, high power rockets are larger and require careful design to ensure safety in flight. As the impulse of the motor increases, more advanced systems are required necessitating avionics to manage parachute deployment and tracking, composites, and flight simulation. High power rocketry is an accessible way to learn these skills that are applicable to many other areas in Aerospace Engineering.
