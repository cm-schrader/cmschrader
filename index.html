NASA Student Launch Initiative

WPI AIAA
Captain
2019 - 2020 Competition Year
Safety Officer
2018 - 2019 Competition Year
Co-Founder
2018

Towards the end of my sophmore year, I joined up with two other students to found a NASA Student Launch Initiative Team as a subcomitee of the WPI Student Chapter of the AIAA. While the club had competed in rocketry competitions before, Student Launch was a far greater challenge.

The competition involves designing, testing, and building a rocket to fly as close as possible to a target altitude, typically around one mile. After landing, it must deploy a payload to complete a specific task such as taking a soil sample.

To compete, teams must respond to NASA's RFP within the first month of school. Teams that are accepted must complete four design reviews which ussualy take the form of 150+ page technical reports followed by a conference with NASA.

Our first year, I filled the critical role of Safety Officer. In this posistion, I was responsible for minimizing risk to people, property, and facilities throughout the entire process. I also managed the project's risk analysis and operation procedures in our design reviews.

This year, I have been appointed Captain. I'm responsible for heading an Officer Board of 9 other Officers and leading the entire team consisting of another ### regular Members. Even before the start of the competition, I've set our team up for success by creating official procedures and explicit expectations for my Officers and Members. Thanks to the many lessons learned our first year, WPI is poised to be a highly competitive team.

HERO FireBot

Aeromechanics Divison, NASA Ames Research Center
Intern Group Lead
June 3rd to August 9th, 2019

In the summer of 2019, I interned at NASA Ames. My mentor assigned me to work on the new HERO Project, which focuses on applications of rotorcraft in natural disasters. Of the two concepts being developed for the project, I was appointed Lead for the FireBot group, which consisted of two other collage interns and one high school intern, and the point of contact for DemoBot, the other group.

The team was given a simple goal: "Create a concept for a multirotor UAV that helps fight wildland fires and develop a proof-of-concept robot." We researched the problems that wildland fire fighters face to see where UAV technology could help. The problem we selected was extinguishing spot fires, small fires created when embers blow over a fire break that break the fire's containment if left uncontroled. Due to their small size, it can be hard to find and extinguish them.

Because other groups have already been developing UAVs for locating spot fires, we decided to take on the challenge of extinguishing them Once a spot fire is located, our concept UAV, FireBot, would be dispatched to its location. After getting close to it by using GPS, FireBot finds the fire's exact posistion using IR sensors before deploying its fire suppressant payload and returning to its base station. From the base station, it would be restocked and recharged before going onto standby until its next sortie.

To demonstrate the feasibility of the concept, the group constructed a low cost, proof-of-concept robot based on Arduino. It successfully completed simulated sorties where it drove to designated coordinates before persuing an IR source and deploying suppressant on it. On the group, I was in charge of writing the robot's code, including its coordinate navigation, IR tracking, and state machine.

Featured Work

Busek Co. Inc.

Busek is a small research company in Natick, Massachusets, which develops electric propulsion and other systems for spacecraft. My internship was split between working with the design group and the electrical engineering group.

In the design group, I primarily worked to revise Solidworks parts, drawrings, and assemblies working with other engineers and with vendors. In addition, I also created spreadsheets in Excel for inventory tracking and built an electrical filter for use in a test setup. I completed my planned work in the design group one month ahead of schedule.

In the electrical engineering group I worked to improve the company's PCB prototyping process. I wrote procedures to encompass the full process which included, exporting designs from Eagle, CNC milling, applying soldermask, and tinning. In addition, I also designed and fabricated an aluminum vacuum chuck to hold down stock for milling PCBs. This significantly reduced the amount of time spent securing stock and prevented it from warping. I also coded a data aquisistion and visualization Python program. It connects to a current meter over ethernet and records, plots, and does basic analysis on data. Finally, I populated circuit boards that were used by the testing group.
