Mechanical Design

Components and Assemblies

Pictured: The motor and fin mount of my L2 certification rocket, Sepatron.

The quintessential engineering skill, mechanical design converts ideas of how a system should function to defined part designs. Naturally as an aerospace engineer, many of my projects involve some sort of component design. There are many details that must be considered when making parts. Parts need to be designed with more than just function in mind. The limits of your manufacturing, materials, assembly, and budget need to be considered.

The Solidworks assembly model of my solder fume extraction fan.

I've applied my skills on projects big and small. One of example of this was the solder fume extraction fan I built. Tired of trying to avoid inhaling solder fumes, I decided to build my own using a fan and power switch I salvaged from a broken ATX power supply along with a cheap active carbon filter and a 12 V DC power supply. After assembling the electrical part of the system to verify I could switch the fan and an indicator LED on and off, I designed a simple enclosure for the fan and filter. I created a stand with joints toleranced to be easy to rotate but still able to hold their angle. After 3D printing the parts, the assembled project worked perfectly and didn't require any fasteners. This was a simple project, but my understanding of tolerances, electronics, and manufacturing enabled me to complete a quality project quickly.
