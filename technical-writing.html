Technical Writing

Descriptive and Persuasive Technical Reports

Pictured: Page 13 of the FireBot Paper I co-authored at NASA Ames. The figure shows the output of IR sensor array used on our robot.

Being able to write good documentation and reports for technical projects is important for communicating the efficacy of a design and its rationale to people who aren't necessarily working on the project. Before going into engineering, I had a good writing foundation writing essays for my history and philosophy courses. I have written a variety of technical reports in different projects and served as Documentation Officer on both my software engineering team and USLI. I strive to keep my documentation concise but descriptive with consistent formatting and professional language. These skills also extend to code documentation. I have made frequent use to formats such as Doxygen and Javadoc in the majority of my projects.
