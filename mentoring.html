Teaching New Engineers and Passing On Skills

Pictured: A screen capture taken from a high power rocketry workshop that I (top right) ran for USLI members during the summer of 2020.

Engineering is best as a team activity. For a team to run well, members need to be on the same page and have the skills to contribute. When I'm new to a team, I'm able to pick up skills efficiently but I also have experience in helping out other new members. I have experience tutoring, running workshops, and otherwise teaching other engineers new skills. I've tutored for WPI's Astronautics course with SGT as well as worked on a group to create a MATLAB workshop for new aerospace students. I also spent time mentoring the high school intern who was assigned to my group during my internship at NASA Ames. Finally, myself and some other upper class students have run workshops for USLI to help members gain the skills they need to contribute to the team.
