Project Management

Time and Requirements Organization in Personal and Team Projects

Pictured: Part of a Gantt Chart used by USLI.

While everyone wants to design an optimal system, this is rarely practical in real life. Projects almost always have budget and time constraints. The ability to manage these is vital as is being able to react to changes to the project. I've used many different methods to do this such as Gantt charts, internal deadlines, and progress reviews.

On a small scale, I've effectively managed my course work on WPI's fast moving 7-week quarter system as well as individual intern projects such as the ones I completed at Busek. Beyond individual work, I've managed many group projects such as the FireBot project during my time at NASA Ames. Most notably, I served as Captain of USLI where I lead 44 general members and 9 officers to compete in a NASA competition, meeting all of our project deadlines.
